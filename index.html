HISTORY

Women Across Borders started in 2008 as response to the xenophobic attacks targeted towards refugees and foreign nationals in South Africa in May of that year. 62 people died in these attacks, the majority of whom were women and children. One of the main reasons why refugees were affected by xenophobia was the misconception which still continues around the globe that refugees take jobs and resources from the local communities.

Women Across Borders responded to this by establishing an adult literacy programme aimed at teaching refugee women how to read, write and speak English as a means to enable their integration into society. Language is one of the crucial factor in a refugee’s life determining how integrated they will be in the community and what resources they will be able to access. Refugee women not only face language difficulties, but unlike their male partners who are presented with more opportunities to socialize and learn the local language and find employment; refugee women often face unemployment, poverty, domestic violence and trauma hence the incorporation of the skills development and advocacy project in our response to the struggles of refugee women.

Education

We sponsor refugee girls to go back to school and build the foundation to a successful life. We offer adult learning opportunities for refugee women who are considerably old to return to high school or never completed their high school education.

Tech Skills

The future of work is technology, and refugee women and girls should not be left behind. We teach refugee women how to code.

Business Development

We help refugee women develop business plans and source funding for small scale businesses that meet a specific need in their community. These businesses also have the potential to create jobs for other refugee women.

Advocacy

We empower refugee women and girls with knowledge of their rights and encourage them to speak up and become agents of justice, for the many millions of women and girls whose voice we may never hear.
